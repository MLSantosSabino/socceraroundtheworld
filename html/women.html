Marta, whose full name is Marta Vieira da Silva, is a Brazilian football legend widely regarded as one of the greatest female players in the history of the sport. Born on February 19, 1986, in Dois Riachos, Brazil, Marta's remarkable career has been characterized by her exceptional skill, agility, and goal-scoring prowess. Often referred to as the "Queen of Soccer," Marta has represented Brazil in multiple FIFA Women's World Cups and Olympic Games, earning accolades for her outstanding performances. She has been awarded the FIFA World Player of the Year six times, a testament to her consistency and impact on the pitch. Marta has played for various clubs, both domestically and internationally, including FC Rosengård in Sweden and the Orlando Pride in the National Women's Soccer League (NWSL). Beyond her on-field achievements, Marta has been an influential figure in promoting women's football and advocating for gender equality in the sport. Her legacy extends far beyond her numerous records and titles, making Marta an inspirational figure for aspiring footballers globally.
